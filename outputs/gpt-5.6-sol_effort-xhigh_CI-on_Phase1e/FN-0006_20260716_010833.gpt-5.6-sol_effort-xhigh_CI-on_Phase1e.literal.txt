    Field notes 6

    **The imprisonment of Farajallah**. After this Salāmih ar-Rṃāg said that
he should become śēx. Old Sālim agreed. They went to Mḥi, but he said that
Sālim should remain śēx.
    Salāmih was very angry, and as a result denounced Farajallah, evidently
with Ṣāliḥ's help. Farajallah was imprisoned for about a month, and beaten.
    When he got out he demanded a ḥagg from Salāmih (who is his xāḷ).
It was agreed that Salāmih should yalḥas an-nāṛ. He gave Slēm ar-Rṃāg as
kifīl that if he didn't do so, then he'd ya‘ṭiy al-ḥagg. They agreed to meet
chez ‘Awwād al-Grayniy in al-‘Arīś tom make the arrangements, and get per-
mission.
    In the event Salāmih didn't turn up. Farajallah went to Salāmih and
demanded a ḥagg. He was refused. He then turned to Slēm, and was also re-
fused. But he didn't blacken.

    **Ṣāliḥ threatens Farajallah**. One day at the Kuntillih well Ṣāliḥ drew
his dagger on Farajallah. The case came before Ibn Raḍ̄wān and he had to pay
50 pounds.

    **Ṣāliḥ stabs Sālim**. One day these two were at the well, and young
Msallam was drawing water. Ṣāliḥ told him not to splash water on him as
he brought the bucket up, and threatened to throw him into the well. Sālim
reproached Ṣāliḥ, and the latter stabbed Sālim high on the left shoulder.
Sālim didn't get to strike back. Ṣāliḥ went off toget his rifle, but by-
standers prevented anything happening. Sālim was ill for about seven months
in al-‘Arīś.
    The Rumgān got an ‘aṭwa, but only from the ‘Tāygih, not from the
Farajāt. This is odd, since the Mṭūr are a single CLG, and Sālim says that
any blood money they got would be shared among them all. He's not sure what
share he'd have got as the person who suffered the wound - maybe a quarter.
    Old Sālim went to Mḥi ar-Rṃāg to try to keep things under control - he
feared there'd be a killing. He told him not to interfere, and said that he
Sālim would if necessary pay the gaṣṣ out of his own pocket. Mḥi refused.
Sālim also proposed that the parties should each use only one side of the K.
well - the Mṭūr the lower one, the Rumgān the upper. This seems to have
been accepted.

    **Killing of Ṣāliḥ**: one day Faraj came down to the well and found Ṣāliḥ
sitting on the Mṭūr side of it. Ṣāliḥ had no flocks with him. Faraj was in
the ḥaras and had a rifle. He told Ṣāliḥ to get up. Ṣāliḥ said he'd only
leave if Faraj said yal‘an abūk. Faraj said this, and Ṣāliḥ drew his pistol
and shot at Faraj. He missed, and Faraj shot him back and killed him. He
then fled. This occurred some 4 or 5 days after the death of Jamāl ‘Abd-an-
Nāṣir.
