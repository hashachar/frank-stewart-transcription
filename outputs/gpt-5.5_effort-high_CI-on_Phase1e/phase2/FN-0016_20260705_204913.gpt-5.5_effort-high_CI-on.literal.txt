Field notes 16

**Saturday 25 Nov. 1978**
    I worked in the clinic. The Xanāṭlih who had failed to be at our
rendevous in Elat now turned it. In the night, after I'd had dinner with
them, the police had come and imprisoned them for not having permission to
spend the night in Elat. They were only released around 3 pm, after śēxs
had given guarantees for them, and they then came back on foot.
    I forgot to mention that Slēmān Ibn Aḥamad is now in hospital in Elat.
I went to visit him, and he's very ill.
    At 4.00 left with SH to sēl al-Jihayyir where were śēx Slēmān and sons.
Recorded talk on tapes no 140-141. At 9.00 went to read in the jeep, and
at 11.00 to sleep. Spent a cold, restless night.

**Sunday 26 Nov. 1978**
**The Ms vs the Ns,** cf.
    Up at 7.00 and at 8.00 set out with SH and Faṛṛāj to Kuntillih to try
to settle this problem. We arrived at the Ms in W. al-Xamīlih at 10.00, but
found only Slēmān b. ‘Iliy on the spot. While we were there Frayj b. Mḥaysin
arrived in his lorry. We eat, left at 11.30 and at 12.30 arrived at the
Kuntillih well. Here we heard further news of confiscated stock: six flocks
(ġanamāt) were found by the Green Patrols over the border last week: those
of Hsēn ‘Id, al-‘Awdiy, b. Sa‘ad, śēx Jim‘ih, Mṭayrih (wife of ‘Awdih) of
the Kaṛādmih, and one other I've forgotten. It seems the flocks have
all been taken to Beer Sheva, and will only be released on payment of heavy
fines.

    We learnt that the Nṣ were in Tlē‘it Saṛṛāh, a small wadi (ṭal‘ih)
that flows into the Jirāfiy from the W opposite Gōz Aṃṃ Ḥśēśāt. Here
the three brothers are camped with ‘Iliyān b. al-‘Umdih and Sallām ‘Iliyān
(and possibly someone else - maybe ‘Iliyān Ibn Raḍwān). A large comfortable
camp with several tents, and a proper Beḍouin guest tent - made out of the
traditional materials and divided into two parts, in one of which we sat.
Without going into details of the accusations made, SH proposed that the
parties meet in a bēt malamm, and try to sort things out. He represented
himself as the intermediary. The Nṣ - the two older brothers were already
there, and ‘Iliy turned up in the course of our talk - agreed. (‘Iliy had
been driving ‘Id ‘Awdih's lorry in W. Waṭīr and broke a spring; ‘Id has
gone to al-‘Arīś, and ‘Iliy hired a pickup/[?]an-Nīzān's nephew to
get him home.) At my suggestion (opposed by Faṛṛāj, as usual) all three
came with us in the jeep in another attempt to find ‘Iliyān Ibn Mismiḥ.
This time we did find him at his home, and since he said he could stand in
for his son, we then all went back again to the Kuntillih well, where ar-
Ṛmāg was waiting for us (he'd been warned by us we might come).
    We sat down by a bush about 4.45. Two men
were already there: Msallam Abu Ṛās and Salīm Ibn Warrād. The latter had
maybe come in connexion with the renxewal of the ‘aṭwa between the Hs and
the Brēkāt.
