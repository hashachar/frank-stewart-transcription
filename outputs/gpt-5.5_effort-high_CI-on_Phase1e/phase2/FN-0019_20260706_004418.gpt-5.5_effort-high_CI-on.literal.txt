29 Nov to 2 Dec 1978                                      Field notes 19

**29 Nov. 1978, Wednesday**
    Up at 7.15, and at 8.00 Slēm Gaṃṃāṛ turned up. Some years ago he was
lent or given some kind of explosive (kuḥl) by Faṛṛāj 'Iliyān for the pur-
pose of making a cistern. (It seems that he never made it, and still has
the stuff - left by the Egyptian army - stored away somewhere.) Yesterday,
just before we set off with the water to his home, he quarrelled with
young 'Id Farrāj about the matter, 'Id claiming it was a loan, and Slēm a
gift. The quarrel was very bitter, each side blackening the other, and
'Iliyān Slēmān also took a small part. This morning, it seems, Farrāj turned
up and the matter continued. Slēm now asked me to take him to Ḥsēn 'Āyid
to consult him, and I did. We found Swēriy Abut Gardūd with Ḥsēn 'Āyid.
Ḥsēn's reaction was to call Slēm a liar to his face, thus confirming what
SH had already said to me - that Slēm had a poor reputation even among the
Xanāṭlih.
    Bakc to the clinic at 10.00, but what with one thing and another
didn't do any work till about 4.00. SH had a very busy day, since various
lorries were around: Ibrahīm 'Abd al-'Āṭiy came in from as-Sawrah, loaded
up from stuff he'd left here, and went back again; Abu Gardūd came to take
water to the lower W. 'Agābah; and the 'Tāygih lorry was also around. The
'Tāygih are building a little shop to rival SH's, also close to the well.
SH and Ibrahīm have already worked out how to drive it out of business in
a few months. SH is quite amused by the whole thing, not at all irritated,
and his relations with his friend Sālim Ibn 'Tayyig are not affected.

**Thursday 30 Nov 1978**
    Worked in clinc, stayed over at night when SH and Ḥsēn took jeep to
Ṭarfa. SI, now living on his own in Ṭubayg, took the Volvo back to al-
'Arīś. When we stopped over in sēl al-Jihayyir on Monday there was talk
about SI's mother, and what SH had said about her being disliked and about
SI now having to live on his own was confirmed.

**Friday 1 Dec. 1978**
    Up at 7.00, and from 8.00 to 12.00 worked with SH. Then Michel Z.
from the Red cross arrived, and I spent the rest of the day with him,
explaining all the various problems, including those that may arise if there
is a peace treaty with Egypt. We had dinner with Ḥsēn 'Āyid. Slept in the
clinic.

**Saturday 2 Dec 1978**
    Up at 7.00, and at 9.00 the Red Cross left, taking with him the wife
of 'Id Abu Graygānih who lives in Gaza. She had come here on a visit to
her relatives (I think she is the Z of 'Id b Sallām). Went for a walk,
took more water to Slēm Gaṃṃāṛ. Tried to get genealogical data from his
old mother, but it was a total flop: she was evasive and even lied.
