24 Jan. 1979                                             field notes      23

**Wednesday 24 Jan. 1979**
    Left Elat at 11.00 with a lot of vegetables, a couple of frozen
chickens, clothes from WIZO and one or two other odds and ends.
Stopped at Ras an-Nagb, but no-one around except the old Naxlawiy ‘Aṭiyyih,
who was watering a camel I think, and with him another old man.
    Reached Thamad at 2.00 to find only Ḥsēn and Muṣṭafa the teacher.
Turnts out that SH yesterday left with his wife Frayjih in the doctor's
jeep. Her eye trouble got much worse, and he decided the time had come
to get her proper attention. His flocks are left in charge of SI's sister
Faḍiyyih and little ‘Idih, and as it later turned out, SH's worries
about the effect of Frayjih leaving her home were justified:
about half a dozen small stock were lost to wolves one night.
    I learnt that SH had got very worried by my protracted absence. I'd
said to him that I expected to return in a couple of weeks, but as usual
grossly underestimated the amount of time the work would take; and also,
as often before, found that if I give what I intend to be a vague state-
ment about a length of time, it tends to be taken more literally than I
intend it. In the end SH had contacted Lezer and Lezer had tried to find
me, even checking hospitals etc. I felt rather guilty about the whole
thing.
    I decided that the best way to use the time under the circumstances
would be to go out to Bīr Mālḥih and stay with Daxaḷaḷḷah. Before
going, however, I wanted to have a word with Sālim ‘Iliy, just to see
if there was anything interesting in the air, and with ‘Iliyān Ḥamdān,
to check the latest developments in the dispute with the Jirāydih, which
I'd already heard about from Sālim Slēm in Elat (see below). Both SI
and ‘Iliyān were camped a little north of the well, and I went to
.visit them with Sālim ‘Tayyig and his uncle Slēmān Ḥsēn (they are still
running their shop, but apparently with little success; their
lorry was stuck in Aṃṃ al-Bārid). SI said there was nothing
scheduled to happen round here`that he knew of, and ‘Iliyān the same.
To my relief I haven't missed anything of interest during my long
absence, excepting only the Jirāydih business, which I don't think
matters, since I expect there to be a further meeting.
    Back to Thamad and sleep in the clinic.

**Awlād Ḥamdān vs Jirāydih**
    A couple of the Njūm - I think ‘Iliy Ḥamdān plus a couple of
younger ones, maybe Mḥa Sālim and one other - went over to Bīr Mālḥih
looking for five lost camels. They'd heard they were watering
at Aṃṃ Si‘īd. No sign of them there, so they assumed they'd been taken
by the Jirāydih. They therefore grabbed a couple of Jirāydih camels.
