Field notes 20

**Wuday al-Bēḍ̄**
    I got back to Thamad at 1.30, and found that Farrāj ‘Id and the Tarabīn
had arrived in a jeep. This was as expected: they were supposed to come the
day of the ṣadagah, originally fixed for today, but then postponed to to-
morrow. Five other Turbānis had come with Farrāj; the only one I knew was
Mha b. Sālāmih.
    When I arrived they were sitting in ‘Iliyān's new stone hut - a fine
piece of work, with even a concrete floor - and nothing of significance
was said till around 6.00. In the meantwhile SH had brough śēx Slēmān, who
was not far away, having come in for the ṣadagah, and a lot of other Aḥaywis
turned up in the course of the evening: inter alia, ‘Id ‘Iliyān (up from
Elat for the ṣadagah), Slēmān b. Ḥ. Slēm, the two ‘Ṭāygih brothers,
‘Iliy Ḥamdān, Slēmān Ḥamdān, Ḥsēn ‘Ayid, and Sālim Slēmān (Ns),who arrived
just now from B. al-Māliḥ. By the end of the evening the hut was extremely
crowded. I brought my paraffin lamp in, there was a good fire, and so
conditions were unusually comfortable. The talks continued till midnight.
    SH tried throughout to represent the Gs, and to prevent śēx Slēmān
from saying anything. He almost succeeded, but at one point the old man
managed to get some ill timed words in and called Farrāj a liar. For this
he had to pay a ḥagg, with ‘Iliyān Ḥamdān (qua rā‘ al-bēt?) acting as
judge: Farrāj was awarded riḅā‘ and riba‘iyyih, which of course he
eventually forwent.
    The tone of the evening was extremely heated, with Farrāj and SH doing
most of the talking (or rather: shouting). Farrāj tried to get the Gṣār to
go to court with him, I think in order to get them to give a formal declar-
ation that the Ḥs are on their own vis-a-vis the ‘Arāyḍ̄ah (tabwīg). SH
argued back that (a) there is no reason for the Gs qua śexs to intervene,
since there is the kull xamsih b-xamsitha agreement. He accepts only what-
ever responsibilities in the dispute fall to any other rub‘ of the Aḥaywāt.
If the Tarabīn want to take the Gs to court about this, then they must
bring along a śēx. The Gs absolutely,refuse to go to court against Farrāj.
The latter's feeble attempts to argue that he was a śēx were easily beaten
down. (b) That in any case, was Farrāj must now do is to make three badwixhs
against the Ḥs and put the śāhid ad-dbāriy on them; only then does the
question of the Gs intervening even arise.
    Various secondary disputes arose in the course of the evening. (1) The
dispute for which old śēx Slēmān was fined, as mentioned above. (2) Ḥsēn
b. ‘Ayid at one point took a line which very much didn't appeal to SH,and
the latter even claims that he proposed to Farrāj that he make a badwih

but to go to court with him. On the morning of Dec 4 there was (in my
