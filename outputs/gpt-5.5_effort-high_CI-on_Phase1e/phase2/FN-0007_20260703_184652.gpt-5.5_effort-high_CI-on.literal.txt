    Field notes 7

    The ‘Tāygih fled to Thamad, where the Gṣāṛ were, and old Sālim went up
to Nixl to report what had happened. Salāmih also went; he said all the
‘Tāygih had been involved - they all had guns and had shot at Ṣāliḥ. The
‘Tāygih denied this entirely, and said, "Is it likely we'd come to surrender
ourselves if we were responsible?" Only Faraj was involved.
    The government imprisoned Sālim, Msallam, and Slēmān Ḥsēn, but not old
Sālim. They were kept in jail some three and a half months, but were not
beaten (except that an officer slapped one of them when he came to them
with Salāmih). Various śexs - al-Gṣayyir, Ibin ‘Āmir, and others - then
guaranteed them, and theywere released.


    **The śexship.** About four months before he died, when he was already ill,
old Sālim chose Slimān Ḥsēn to succeed him. His family remained in al-‘Arīṣ,
but he would come down to the Aḥaywāt.
    At the time of the śīk Sālim and Msallam were taken in foar a couple
of hours, Slimān and Farajallah for longer. Slimān was deposed from the
śexship, and the Rumgān made ‘Iliy an-Nhāriy śex. They didn't make Mḥi śex
since they knew that none of the leading Aḥaywāt would co-operate with him.

    **Ṣāliḥ stabs Sālim (cont.) and other blows.** There was no gaṣṣ, and about
7 months passed between the stabbing and Ṣāliḥ's death. The toing and froing
and the two rib‘ān mentioned on 3/180 are apparently connected with some
quite different dispute between the parties - Ṣāliḥ hit Slimān Ḥsēn, and the
latter hit Mḥi.

    **Ṭulū‘.** It seems Mḥi is prepared to allow a ṭulū‘ only (a) if it is
confined to ‘Id's sons (so SH), and (b) it does not cover the
Kuntillih area - which is the area they really want to go to, since they
were always there and their mazāri‘ are there.
    Another complication is ‘Id. It  seems Mḥi says he must either come
himself, or give his consent. Getting his consent is not so simple, since
a suitable messenger must be found to go to him to explain things (so Sālim).
‘Id is scared that if he comes the Israelis will imprison him - Sālim thinks
this fear is unbased.
    SH tells me that some while back Sālim and Msallam wanted to ṭili‘
independently of their uncle Slimān; but Sālim nows says that they must
all ṭili‘ together (from the Farajāt).
    Sālim sends emissaries from time to time, e.g. Faṛṛāj ‘Id the Turbāniy,
Wdayyān, Sālim Sallām, and Ibin Aḥamad, who was the last one, and went some
four months ago.
