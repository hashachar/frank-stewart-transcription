Field notes     9

**Bilād aś-Ṣgēliy** 28-29Nov 78. From Ḥsēn ‘Āyid and Slēm Ṣāliḥ
    There is a Xanṭally rub‘ called the Ṣagalah (ancestors aś-Ṣgēliy), who
long ago - Ḥsēn ‘Āyid says before the Suez canal was cut - moved to Egypt..
I have discovered no trace of them in my genealogies, but both Ḥsēn and
Swēriy Abu Gardūd (who was with him) say that they have met them, been enter-
tained by them, etc. They are pretty much sedentarized.
    The ancestor of the group - aś-Ṣgēliy - claimed some land as
ḥajar in what is now the country of the Ṣafāyḥih. This was long, long ago -
Ḥsēn says before the Aḥaywāt started to cultivate W. al-‘Aqābah. The land
seems to be in a wadi called W. al-Bayyāḍ, which is marked on the 1:250,000
map, but (according to what Slēm Ṣāliḥ says) erroneously. It flows into
W. al-Jīḍ‘. Ḥsēn gave me the borders of the Ṣgēliy land thus: ḥadīdha min
taḥat sēl Abu Swēraḥ is Abu Raṣāṣah (Ṣaṛ.). Min fōg yḥiddha min bāb aṣ-ṣalāh
al-Mrayr (which is a θamīlih). To the east, malagit al-Jīḍ‘, (malagīh means
something like ‘irg, a patch of ground which is solid stone, as if
pavedx) and Ṭawr al-Hdiy. Min tagrīb xālaṣ - qanāt aś-Ṣgēliy. Min śmāl,
nawāḥiy bdi‘ - qabr williy f-al-biyyāḍ. aṣ-ṣufr w-al-Biyyāḍ w-abu Swēraḥ -
all the area thus bounded belonged to aś-Ṣgēliy.
    It seems that before 1967 a few Xanāṭlih would from time to time cultiv-
ate a bit of this land. At one time the Ṣagalah themselves would come, but
they've long ceased to do so. Ḥsēn says he twice planted sayfiy there, the
second time just before the troubles to be described.
    It seems that in about 1966 several people decided to plant, I think,
sayfiy there because of drought round here. Among them were Ḥsēn ‘Āyid,
Swēriy, Slēm Ṣāliḥ, Xmayyis Ibn Darwīś, Salmān Ṣāliḥ and maybe ‘Id Ibn Miṣliḥ.
Perhaps also Ḥ. Mḥi Abu Gardūd. There seems to have been trouble for some
time with the Ṣafāyḥih about this land, and Ḥsēn mentions a mi‘ād which took
place before this chez Abu Mṣēfir (Ḥwēṭāt) at which the Xanāṭlih - represented
by Sālim ‘Ayid and Ḥmēd Ibn Jim‘iḥ - got a 20 pound fine from the Ṣafāyḥih
because (I think) they had cultivated the land.
    On this occasion the Ṣafāyḥih broke the wire fences round the cultivated
patches and allowed their camels to eat the young crop. Ḥsēn says there was
no mi‘ād afterwards, though he talked to the Ṣafāyḥih about the matter in
the market in al-‘Arīṣ. No-one was beaten. Slēm says mi‘āds were made with
the Ṣafāyḥih, but they just didn't turn up; and that Ḥ. Mḥi ran thngs for
the Xanāṭlih. Ḥsēn says that if the Xanāṭlih now wanted to cultivate the
land, they could do so and there would be no trouble.
    Slēm describes the land as not "improved" (mṣallaḥ), and says that the
Xanāṭlih intended to divide it among themselves - among all the Xanāṭlih -
equally after this ill-fated season. He gives a picture of the Xanāṭlih dec-
iding suddenly to take this land seriously, and from then on make consider-
able use of it. I am not at all sure that this is correct.
