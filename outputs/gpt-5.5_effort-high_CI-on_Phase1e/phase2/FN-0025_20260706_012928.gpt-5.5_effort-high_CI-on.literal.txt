**Thursday 25 Jan. 1979**
    Up at 7.45 feeling refreshed. Packed the jeep with lots of petrol
etc., had breakfast with Ḥsēn at 9.00. Muṣṭafa has lost his good habit
of starting teaching at the proper time, and is now behaving like ‘Abid
used. (When Salāmih got back from al-‘Arīs he took Muṣṭafa aside and
told him he must pull his socks up; that if he didn't he'd complainx
to Lezer; but that if he did, the matter would be forgotten. The threat
was effective.) Sālim ‘Iliy and śēx Slēmān's wife came down to the
well.

    Left (on my own) at 10.00, reached Naxl at 12.30 and got a puncture
mended. Lezer was not around. Into Bīr Mālḥih at 3.30, where I spoke to
‘Abdallah the teacher, who was very helpful. He gave me one of Dax's sons,
Jim‘ih, to take me back to Dax's home. When we got there we learnt that
Dax and Mḥi his brother are down at Aṃṃ Sa‘īd waiting for some camels
to come to water. In contrast to Mālḥih, where the water has to be pulled
up in buckets, and where few camels come, at Aṃṃ Sa‘īd there is some
kind of pool from which they can drink on their own.

    Jim‘ih is a bright lad of xmaybe 8, and at dusk he brought me neatly
to where Dax and Mḥi had their ‘izbih. I got a good welcome. Dax at
first wanted to hand me over to Mḥi - I don't know how seriously - but
I told him I wanted to ask him about the ‘urfiy. He and I and young Jim‘ih
then went back to his home for the night.

    Dax is camped about 15 kms from B. Mālḥih, in the W. al-‘Arīs, in
the same place that he was at the time of the mi‘ād of the hijacked lorry.
I think he's been there the whole time since. He said himself that he
stays a year or two in one place, and so the only person camped with him
is his son-in-law ‘Id Sālim (who is working in Israel at the moment).
Dax's elder wife, Fāṭmih Ṭrayfiy (Ḥs) is in this home. The younger
wife, Sālmih Ṣāliḥ, is camped a short way from Mālḥih and looks after
the children who are in the school. Dax owns, I believe, 5 camels, but
apart from that only perhaps half a dozen sheep and goats and a few
chxickens. He seems to live off his legal earnings plus money sentto
him by the older sons who work in Israel.
