**The expensive dinner** (from SI)
    I finally got an account of thef facts in this case that made sense.
    Sālim Slāmān (SS) and Faḍiyyih Ḥamdān, mother of Sālim ‘Id (Ns), had
homes next to each other at the film.

    One evening a ġmās was made in SS's home. A lot of people came for
dinner, so he said to put some of the ġmās in a separate bowl for the two
women - his wife Sa‘Īdih and his sister. They took the bowl, and some bread,
and went to eat in Sa‘Īdih's mother's home.

    The men finished the ġmās, but SS was still hungry, so he went over to
his mother-in-law Faḍiyyih's home to eat with the women. It seems, however,
that they had given the bowl of ġmās to Slimān b. Sa‘ad
and Slimān Ibin Mismiḥ. Certainly, when SS came he found the two women -
Faḍiyyih was not with them, being it seems in SS's own home - eating only
bread, with no bowl of ġmās in sight. He asked them where it was, they said
it was in the dāraṃ. They evidently thought b. Sa‘ad had put the bowl
back in the ṣīrih - but he hadn't, he'd left it outside.

    As a result SS then took his sister outside and beat her. Sa‘Īdih
stayed in the bēt, wasn't beaten, and fled then to śēx Jim‘ih.
    Within a day or two SS went to Jim‘ih's home and there was talk about
the matter. Ibn Sa‘ad denied any parti in the matter.
    Sa‘Īdih later moved from Jim‘ih's home to her brothers. They didn't
take any action against Ibin Sa‘ad, and for this reason SI calls them
majāyif xāliṣ. They should have immediately forced Ibin Sa‘ad to go to court,
or else have beaten him.
    At the time of the hearing before ‘Iliyān, Sa‘Īdih was still with her
brothers. She returned to her husband immediately after the mi‘ād.


**‘Tāygih vs. Rumgān.** From Sālim Ibin ‘Tayyig, 18 Nov 1978.
    Among the Ġnaymāt, the first to afrag were the ‘Urjān and Rawasih. Then
there remained one walad who xalaf al-Mṭūr, al-Māgāti‘ih and the Rumgān.
. The two last are equally close to the Mṭūr.
    The CLGs are: ‘Urjān; Rawasih; Migāti‘ih and Rumgān together; and
Mṭūr.
    The Mṭūr are divided into the following: Farajāt, Sbayḥāt, ‘Tāygih, and
the group of which Ṣubḥ Ḥasan Hilayyil - old Ṣubḥ who ṭili‘ - is the only
remaining member. There are no Farajāt or Sbayḥāt in Sinai now. I emphasize:
all the Mṭūr biḥuṭṭuw girś ad-damm ma‘a ba‘aḍhum.
