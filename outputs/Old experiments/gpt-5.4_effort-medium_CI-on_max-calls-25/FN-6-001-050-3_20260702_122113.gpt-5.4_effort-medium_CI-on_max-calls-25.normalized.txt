**Salmān Sālim's marriages** (from SI)


A. Salmān Sālim Salmān is šēx Jimʿih's brother. He has an ʿamm, ʿAwdih
Salmān, who is in Jordan. ʿAwdih's son, Mḥa, married Salmān's daughter,
Nāfilih; I'm not whether this was before or after ʿAwdih left for
Jordan (his departure was after 1967), but I think before. It was agreed
that Salmān would take ʿAwdih's daughter ʿYēdih in exchange for Nāfilih,
but at the time of Mḥa's marriage ʿYēdih was apparently not yet nubile.
    Some time after his father moved to Jordan, Mḥa tried to cross over
there (to visit him, to join him?). He was killed in the attempt, leaving
a daughter.

    At the time of Mḥa's death Salmān had still not taken ʿYēdih, but
he had her gaṣalah. One day round Xašm aṭ-Ṭārif, he came to Mtayrih and
her daughter with their ġanam, seized the daughter, and dabaḥ ʿalēha. The
mother went immediately to the air-base and complained. They sent out a
car, but šēx Jimʿih persuaded them that there was nothing for them to do.
B. After Mḥa ʿAwdih's death, his widow Nāfilih returned to her father.
He then gave her to Sālim b. "Ḥmēd" the sculptor, in exchange for Sālim's
sister Ḥamdih (ex-wife of Salāmih ar-Rmāg). Sālim divorced Nāfilih almost
immediately, but Ḥamdih remains with Salmān.

**Salāmih ar-Rmāg vs. Sālim the sculptor** (from SI)
    This occurred after 1967, but long before my arrival. Ḥamdih, at that
time Salāmih's wife, daxalat ʿind axūha, Sālim. She said that her husband
had told her to go to a certain place in the desert where she should
allow certain men to have intercourse with her. Sālim brought in Farrāj ʿId
the Turbāniy as kibīr, and there was a legal case which ended with a manšad
against Salāmih and the divorce of Ḥamdih.

**Sālim ʿIliy pays hagg al-bēt**
    In commenting on the case where he beat an-Nṣēriyyih, SI points out
that he didn't actually beat her--.people separated them quickly. He thinks
the awarded [?] of the double gaṣṣ was fair, and also the 2 [?][?] ribʿān for
seizing her and freeing her, but that the rest was too m high.
