    Field notes 2



The Gṣār said he should go to court, but he did nothing, and so the Gṣār
accepted Frayj without formal agreement that he'd left his previous CLG.
As a result people have been saying ever since then to Frayj and his brothers:
ṭulūʿak raḍīy, since šēx J. didn't kaffal ʿalēkum. SH says this is said
behind the backs of the Gṣār by a number of leading men, including Sālim Ibn
Raḍwān, and adds that if the Karādmih dispute Frayj's allegiance, then they
should go to court.

    Now it seems that the Ḥamadāt have also been looking on Frayj et al.
as Karādmih in this dispute. Exactly what Frayj's motives were in coming on
this occasion to the Gṣār is not clear to me. I would have thought that it
would be in his interests to try to strengthen his ties with them under such
circumstances. He may indeed have demanded that the Gṣār either help, or else
release him from their CLG and allow him to return to Jimʿih's group. SI
thinks that the latter is what he really wanted, and that the Ḥamadāt have
not actually been troubling him.

    At any rate, šēx Slāman freed Frayj from the mḍāmah: he said something
like "ana māṭlig wijhak, w-nāfiḍ ʿilmak", and Frayj said the same to
him. SH says he would have taken money (ribā' aṭ-ṭulū') in these circum-
stances, and considers it likely enough that the greedy (tammā') old šēx
did so, though he didn't actually say he had.

**Frayj b. Mḥaysin vs. šēx Jimʿih**, see 4/237f. From SI.
    The father of the girl - Frayj's brother who lives in Jordan - is called
Sālim. When she daxalat ʿind Jimʿih, SI, šēx ʿIliy, and others came to
Jimʿih's home and raddāwha. She agreed to go back on condition that she live
with her uncle Mḥa (but 4/237 says ʿAwwād). This was accepted, and young Mḥa
b Jimʿih took her to uncle Mḥa.

    When Jimʿih came back and found what had happened he went and recovered
the girl from the two Mḥas. It's not clear what she felt about the matter.
    Frayj came to š. Slāman to complain. SI, Sālim ʿId and Ibn Raḍwān went
to Jimʿih and fixed a bēt malamm, chez Ḥ. Najm. Jimʿih brought Farrāj ʿId the
Turbaniy as his kibīr, and on this occasion Farrāj was warned not to inter-
fere again in the affairs of the Aḥaywāt.

    Frayj got a ḥagg, but SI doesn't remember just what.

**Blackening**
    You say A lahad B b-as-sawdiy, A blackened B; or alhadah b-as-sawdiy,
he blackened him.
