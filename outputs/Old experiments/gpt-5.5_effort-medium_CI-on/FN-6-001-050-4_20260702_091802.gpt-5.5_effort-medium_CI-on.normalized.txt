**The expensive dinner** (from SI)
    I finally got an account of thef facts in this case that made sense.
    S̄lim Slāmān (SS) and Fạiyyih Ḥamdān, mother of S̄lim ʿId (Ns), had
homes next to each other at the film.
    One evening a ġmās was made in SS's home. A lot of people came for
dinner, so he said to put some of the ġmās in a separate bowl for the two
women - his wife SaʿĪdih and his sister. They took the bowl, and some bread,
and went to eat in SaʿĪdih's mother's home.
    The men finished the ġmās, but SS was still hungry, so he went over to
his mother-in-law Fạiyyih's home to eat with the women. It seems, however,
that they had given the bowl of ġmās to Slimān b. Saʿad
and Slimān Ibin Mismiḥ. Certainly, when SS came he found the two women -
Fạiyyih was not with them, being it seems in SS's own home - eating only
bread, with no bowl of ġmās in sight. He asked them where it was, they said
it was in the ḏaṛaṃ. They evidently thought b. Saʿad had put the bowl
back in the ṣīrih - but he hadn't, he'd left it outside.
    As a result SS then took his sister outside and beat her. SaʿĪdih
stayed in the bēt, wasn't beaten, and fled then to šēx Jimʿih.
    Within a day or two SS went to Jimʿih's home and there was talk about
the matter. Ibn Saʿad denied any parti in the matter.
    SaʿĪdih later moved from Jimʿih's home to her brothers. They didn't
take any action against Ibin Saʿad, and for this reason SI calls them
majāyif xāliṣ. They should have immediately forced Ibin Saʿad to go to court,
or else h;ve beaten him.
    At the time of the hearing before ʿIliyān, SaʿĪdih was still with her
brothers. She returned to her husband immediately after the mīʿad.

**ʿTāygih vs. Rumgān.** From S̄lim Ibin ʿTayyig, 18 Nov 1978.
    Among the Ġnaymāt, the first to afrag were the ʿUrjān and Rawasih. Then
there remained one walad who xalaf al-Mṭūr, al-Māgātiʿih and the Rumgān.
The two last are equally close to the Mṭūr.
    The CLGs are: ʿUrjān; Rawasih; Māgātiʿih and Rumgān togeather; and
Mṭūr.
    The Mṭūr are divided into the following: Farajāt, Sbayḥāt, ʿTāygih, and
the group of which Ṣubḥ Ḥasan Hilayyil - old Ṣubḥ who ṭili' - is the only
remaining member. There are no Farajāt or Sbayḥāt in Sinai now. I emphasize:
all the Mṭūr biḥuṭṭuw girš ad-damm maʿa baʿaḏ̣hum.
