**The expensive dinner** (from SI)
    I finally got an account of thef facts in this case that made sense.
Sālim Slāmān (SS) and Faḍiyyih Hamdān, mother of Sālim ‘Id (Ns), had
homes next to each other at the film.
    One evening a gmās was made in SS's home. A lot of people came for
dinner, so he said to put some of the gmās in a separate bowl for the two
women - his wife Sa‘idih and his sister. They took the bowl, and some bread,
and went to eat in Sa‘idih's mother's home.
    The men finished the gmās, but SS was still hungry, so he went over to
his mother-in-law Faḍiyyih's home to eat with the women.* It seems, however,
that they had given the bowl of gmās to Slimān b. Sa‘ad
and Slimān Ibin Mismih. Certainly, when SS came he found the two women -
Faḍiyyih was not with them, being it seems in SS's own home - eating only
bread, with no bowl of gmās in sight. He asked them where it was, they said
it was in the ḍaram. They evidently thought b. Sa‘ad had put the bowl
back in the ṣīrih - but he hadn't, he'd left it outside.
    As a result SS then took his sister outside and beat her. Sa‘idih
stayed in the bēt, wasn't beaten, and fled then to śex Jim‘ih.
    Within a day or two SS went to Jim‘ih's home and there was talk about
the matter. Ibn Sa‘ad denied any parti in the matter.
    Sa‘idih later moved from Jim‘ih's home to her brothers. They didn't
take any action against Ibin Sa‘ad, and for this reason SI calls them
majāyif xālis. They should have immediately forced Ibin Sa‘ad to go to court,
or else h[?]ve beaten him.
    At the time of the hearing before ‘Iliyān, Sa‘idih was still with her
brothers. She returned to her husband immediately after the mi‘ād.

**‘Taygih vs. Rumgān.** From Sālim Ibin ‘Tayyig, 18 Nov 1978.
    Among the Ġnaymāt, the first to afrag were the ‘Urjān and Rawasih. Then
there remained one walad who xalaf al-Mṭūr, al-Migāti‘ih and the Rumgān.
The two last are equally close to the Mṭūr.
    The CLGs are: ‘Urjān; Rawasih; Migāti‘ih and Rumgān together; and
Mṭūr.
    The Mṭūr are divided into the following: Farajāt, Sbayḥāt, ‘Taygih, and
the group of which Ṣubh Hasan Hilayyil - old Ṣubh who ṭili' - is the only
remaining member. There are no Farajāt or Sbayḥāt in Sinai now. I emphasize:
all the Mṭūr bihuṭṭuw girś ad-damm ma‘a ba‘aḍhum.
