**The expensive dinner** (from SI)
    I finally got an account of thef facts in this case that made sense.
Sālim Slāmān (SS) and Faḍiyyih Hamdān, mother of Sālim ʿId (Ns), had
homes next to each other at the film.
    One evening a gmās was made in SS's home. A lot of people came for
dinner, so he said to put some of the gmās in a separate bowl for the two
women - his wife Saʿidih and his sister. They took the bowl, and some bread,
and went to eat in Saʿidih's mother's home.
    The men finished the gmās, but SS was still hungry, so he went over to
his mother-in-law Faḍiyyih's home to eat with the women.* It seems, however,
that they had given the bowl of gmās to Slimān b. Saʿad
and Slimān Ibin Mismih. Certainly, when SS came he found the two women -
Faḍiyyih was not with them, being it seems in SS's own home - eating only
bread, with no bowl of gmās in sight. He asked them where it was, they said
it was in the ḍaram. They evidently thought b. Saʿad had put the bowl
back in the ṣīrih - but he hadn't, he'd left it outside.
    As a result SS then took his sister outside and beat her. Saʿidih
stayed in the bēt, wasn't beaten, and fled then to šex Jimʿih.
    Within a day or two SS went to Jimʿih's home and there was talk about
the matter. Ibn Saʿad denied any parti in the matter.
    Saʿidih later moved from Jimʿih's home to her brothers. They didn't
take any action against Ibin Saʿad, and for this reason SI calls them
majāyif xālis. They should have immediately forced Ibin Saʿad to go to court,
or else h[?]ve beaten him.
    At the time of the hearing before ʿIliyān, Saʿidih was still with her
brothers. She returned to her husband immediately after the miʿād.

**ʿTaygih vs. Rumgān.** From Sālim Ibin ʿTayyig, 18 Nov 1978.
    Among the Ġnaymāt, the first to afrag were the ʿUrjān and Rawasih. Then
there remained one walad who xalaf al-Mṭūr, al-Migātiʿih and the Rumgān.
The two last are equally close to the Mṭūr.
    The CLGs are: ʿUrjān; Rawasih; Migātiʿih and Rumgān together; and
Mṭūr.
    The Mṭūr are divided into the following: Farajāt, Sbayḥāt, ʿTaygih, and
the group of which Ṣubh Hasan Hilayyil - old Ṣubh who ṭili' - is the only
remaining member. There are no Farajāt or Sbayḥāt in Sinai now. I emphasize:
all the Mṭūr bihuṭṭuw girš ad-damm maʿa baʿaḍhum.
