**The stolen corn and the camel**
    One day some Ḥwēṭāt came from Jordan and stole a lot of camels belonging
to the ʿTāygih from W. al-Mhaššam. (This was still before Sālim was born.)
In the W. Grayyih two of these camels
fled from the Ḥwēṭāt. The Brēkāt got hold of one
of these - the ʿTāygih had originally bought it from the ʿUrjān - and re-
fused to give it back, on the grounds that Ṣubḥ had stolen ʿayš from them;
they wanted the camel as compensation.
    ʿTayyig started a case against the Brēkāt. They said, "Bring Ṣubḥ to the
magʿad, and al-ʿAḍaydiy [an ibn ʿamm of the present one] is the kifīl of
your bakrah." (The animal was in the possession of Ibn Warrād - an
ibn ʿamm of the one I've met.) Ṣubḥ refused to appear, and the case was
never settled.
**The šēxship**
    Old Sālim was šēx of the Gnemāt till about 1956, when he retired in
favour of his son ʿId. Mḥi ar-Rmāg had at this time retired from the
police, and wanted himself to be šēx.

    **The false accusation.** On one occasion Mḥi ar-Rmāg accused Slimān Ḥsēn
of entering his house and making love to his (Mḥi's) wife. The wife later
fled to her native tribe, the Mzēnih. It turned out that Mḥi had lied, and
he had to pay a fine to the Mzēnih. Sālim ʿTayyig helped him get her back.
    **}Id moves back and forth.** After the 1967 war ʿId fled to Jordan, and
the then governor of Sinai, "Abu Musiy", made Sālim šēx again (contra ).
Ṣāliḥ b. Mḥi ar-Rmāg then started manoevers to get the šēxdom to the Rumgān,
says Sālim, but this part of the tale is rather unclear.
At any rate, he went over to Jordan, and brought ʿId back, saying he'd get
him made šēxa again. The pair of them went to al-ʿArīš, and here the Israelis
tried to enroll. ʿId in intelligence. He refused, and went back to Jordan
with Ṣāliḥ.
    The two then came back to Sinai a second time (did Ṣāliḥ go a second
time?), Ṣāliḥ threatened to have ʿId imprisoned, and ʿId fled back to
Jordan, where he has remained.
