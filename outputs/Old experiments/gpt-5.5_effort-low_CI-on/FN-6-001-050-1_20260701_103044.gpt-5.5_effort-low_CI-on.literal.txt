Field notes

**14 Nov. 1978**
**The flight of MSālmih b. Mṭēr. Frayj b. Mhayṣin leaves the Gṣār.**
    From SH and SI.
    Mṭēr al-Wēriy belongs to theḤamadāt. He has lots of camels and lives
round Kuntillih. He and his family don't have much to do * with the Gṣār,
and to my knowledge I've never met him. There's no enmity, but Mṭēr is
usually camped far away from the well. His śēx is probably Jim‘ih. It seems
that the only other Ḥamadāt in Sinai are Mismiḥ al-KDēs and his sons, and
(I think) Mhi b. Ḥammād.
    Mṭayr has a daughter called Sālmih, and a son called al-Wēriy, whom
SI says is ahbal. Until the events here recorded, neither had married.
    Jim‘ih recently exchanged one of his daughters - the one who was
married to Mismih, and whose name is probably Mē‘ih - for Sālmih
b. Mṭēr. Jim‘ih himself married Sālmih, and young al-Wēriy got Mē‘ih. This
was about three months ago.
    SI says the idea of Jim‘ih marrying the young girl is crazy. He
already has two wives and a score of dependants. About a month ago Sālmih
fled from him, and no-one has seen her since. The police have been informed.
It's possible that she has crossed, or tried to cross, into Jordan, where
she has an ‘amm called (maybe) ‘Id.
    al-Wēriy and Sālmih have different mothers. al-Wēriy's mother is dead.
He has full siblings who are in Jordan. Sālmih's mother is a Badriyyih. Some-
what before (I think) her daughter's flight, she herself fled to her own
tribe. Apparently al-Wēriy had been causing her troubles. Poor old Mṭēr now
has to look after his small stock himself - apparently there are no other
children in Sinai. al-Wēriy stays all the time by the Kuntillih well in
order to water their camels that come down to drink.

    This episode had led to trouble between Mṭēr's family and the
Kaṛādmih. Even before Sālmih fled, Mṭēr went to śēx Jim‘ih, told him that
the girl wanted to flee, and said that he, Mṭēr,could do nothing about it.
He proposed a double divorce, which Jim‘ih turned down. Mṭēr then told him
that if anything happened to Sālmih - int al-maṭlūb.
    The Ḥamadāt are now saying that they will kill four men of the Kaṛādmih
if the girl has died. (A woman's life is always valued at that of four men.)
Both SI and SH says they have no right to this. SH says that if he were in
the Ḥamadāt's shoes, and the girl turned out to have died,
he would refuse to divorce al-Wēriy's wife, but no more
than that. His refusal would be upheld in court.
    On 14 Nov. 1978 Frayj b. Mhayṣin came to śēx Slimān in sēl al-Jihayyir
in his lorry. With him was Mismih al-KDēs. The source of his troubles is this:
in order to move from one co-liable group to another, you have to have the
formal agreement of both groups. At the time that Frayj joined the Gṣār, the
latter asked śēx Jim‘ih to kaffal that Frayj had left his group. He refused.
