Field notes 1

**14 Nov. 1978**

**The flight of MSālmih b. Mṭēr. Frayj b. Mḥaysin leaves the Gṣār.**
    From SH and SI.

    Mṭēr al-Wēfiy belongs to theḤamadāt. He has lots of camels and lives
round Kuntillih. He and his family don't have much to do [?] with the Gṣār,
and to my knowledge I've never met him. There's no enmixty, but Mṭēr is
usually camped far away from the well. His šēx is probably Jimʿih. It seems
that the only other Ḥamadāt in Sinai are Mismiḥ al-Kbēš and his sons, and
(I think) Mhi b. Hammād.

    Mṭayr has a daughter called Sālmih, and a son called al-Wēfiy, whom
SI says is ahbal. Until the events here recorded, neither had married.
    Jimʿih recently exchanged one of his daughters - the one who was
married to Mismiḥ, and whose name is probably Mēṯih - for Sālmih
b. Mṭēr. Jimʿih himself married Sālmih, and young al-Wēfiy got Mēṯih. This
was about three months ago.

    SI says the idea of Jimʿih marrying the young girl is crazy. He
already has two wives and a score of dependants. About a month ago Sālmih
fled from him, and no-one has seen her since. The police have been informed.
It's possible that she has crossed, or tried to cross, into Jordan, where
she has an ʿamm called (maybe) ʿId.
    al-Wēfiy and Sālmih have different mothers. al-Wēfiy's mother is dead.
He has full siblings who are in Jordan. Sālmih's mother is a Badriyyih. Some-
what before (I think) her daughter's flight, she herself fled to her own
tribe. Apparently al-Wēfiy had been causing her troubles. Poor old Mṭēr now
has to look after his small stock himself - apparently there are no other
children in Sinai. al-Wēfiy stays all the time by the Kuntillih well in
order to water the camels that come down to drink.

    This episode had led to trouble between Mṭēr's family and the
Kaṛādmih. Even before Sālmih fled, Mṭēr went to šēx Jimʿih, told him that
the girl wanted to flee, and said that he, Mṭēr,could do nothing about it.
He proposed a double divorce, which Jimʿih turned down. Mṭēr then told him
that if anything happened to Sālmih - int al-maṭlūb.

    The Ḥamadāt are now saying that they will kill four men of the Kaṛādmih
if the girl has died. (A woman's life is always valued at that of four men.)
Both SI and SH says they have no right to this. SH says that if herx were in
the Ḥamadāt's shoes, and the girl turned out to have died,
he would refuse to divorce al-Wēfiy's wife, but no more
than that. His refused would be upheld in court.

    On 14 Nov. 1978 Frayj b. Mḥaysin came to šēx Slimān in sēl al-Jihayyir
in his lorry. With him was Mismiḥ al-Kbēš. The source of his troubles is this:
in order to move from one co-liable group to another, you have to have the
formal agreement of both groups. At the time that Frayj joined the Gṣār, the
latter asked šēx Jimʿih to kaffal that Frayj had left his group. He refused.
