Field notes 2


The Gṣāṛ said he should go to court, but he did nothing, and so the Gṣāṛ
accepted Frayj without formal agreement that he'd left his previous CLG.
As a result people have been saying ever since to Frayj and his brothers:
ṭulū‘ak raḍīy, since śēx J. didn't kaffal ‘alēkum. SH says this is said
behind the backs of the Gṣāṛ by a number of leading men, including Sālim Ibn
Raḍ̄wān, and adds that if the Kaṛādmih dispute Frayj's allegiance, then they
should go to court.

    Now it seems that the Ḥamadāt have also been looking on Frayj et al.
as Kaṛādmih in this dispute. Exactly what Frayj's motives were in coming on
this occasion to the Gṣāṛ is not clear to me. I would have thought that it
would be in his interests to try to strengthen his ties with them under such
circumstances. He may indeed have demanded that the Gṣāṛ either help, or else
release him from their CLG and allow him to return to Jim‘ih's group. SI
thinks that the latter is what he really wanted, and that the Ḥamadāt have
not actually been troubling him.

    At any rate, śēx Slēmān freed Frayj from the mdamah: he said something
like "ana ṃṭlig wijhak, w-nāfiḍ̄ ‘ilmak", and Frayj said the same to
him. SH says he would have taken money (riḅā‘ aṭ-ṭulū‘) in these circum-
stances, and considers it likely enough that the greedy (tammā‘) old śēx
did so, though he didn't actually say he had.

**Frayj b. Mḥaysin vs. śēx Jim‘ih**, see 4/237f.y From SI.
    The father of the girl - Frayj's brother who lives in Jordan - is called
Sālim. When she daxalat ‘ind Jim‘ih, SI, śēx ‘Iliy, and others came to
Jim‘ih's home and rad̄d̄awha. She agreed to go back on condition that she live
with her uncle Mḥa (but 4/237 says ‘Awwād). This was accepted, and young Mḥa
b Jim‘ih took her to uncle Mḥa.

    When Jim‘ih came back and found what had happened he went and recovered
the girl from the two Mḥas. It's not clear what she felt about the matter.
    Frayj came to ś. Slēmān to complain. SI, Sālim ‘Id and Ibn Raḍ̄wān went
to Jim‘ih and fixed a bēt malamm, chez Ḥ. Najm. Jim‘ih brought Faṛṛāj ‘Id the
Turbāniy as his kibīr, and on this occasion Faṛṛāj was warned not to inter-
fere again in the affairs of the Aḥaywāt.
    Frayj got a ḥagg, but SI doesn't remember just what.

**Blackening**
    You say A lahad B b-as-sawdiy, A blackened B; or alhadah b-as-sawdiy,
he blackened him.
