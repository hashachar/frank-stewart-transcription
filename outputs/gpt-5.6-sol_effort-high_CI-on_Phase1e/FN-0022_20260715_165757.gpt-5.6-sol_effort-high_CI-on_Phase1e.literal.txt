3 Dec 78 to 23 Jan 1979 field notes 22


    ‘Iliy Ḥamdān asked me to take his flour etc. home, and forold time's
sake I agreed, taking ‘Iliyān (though not his ṣadaqah) along as well. ‘Iliy
's stuff was enough to pretty much load the little jeep. Sitting with ‘Iliy
and Sālmih I felt again the poverty of these people.. ‘They are comped near
al-Brēj, ‘Iliy and ‘Iliyān next to each other, Ḥ. Najm close by on his own.
    Back to Thamad at 4.00 on my own, feeling tired. In the evening SH,
śēx Slēmān, Ḥ. Swēlim, Ḥsēn Haykal and part of the time Muṣṭafa
sat and talked; I recorded some stuff about the wicked uncles case.

**Monday 4 Dec. 1978**
    Up at 7.15 and work. Around 2.00 went off to al-Brēj
to pick up ‘Iliy and ‘Iliyān for theṭulū‘. After various complications,
journeys hither and thither, all the Nj were grouped together at the home
of the ‘Tāygih, only a kilometer or so from the Thamad well. Here were:
‘Iliy and ‘Iliyān Ḥamdān, Slēmān b. Slēm (representing his two brothers),
and Mḥa b. Sālim. With the ‘Tāygih we went to SI's home, in the lower
part of W Ṭubayg, also close by, and there the ṭulū‘ was gone through with
SS and Sālim Ibn ‘Tayyig present. In principle it was gone through
again that evening in the ‘Tāygih camp. I went back to the clinic to sleep.

**Tuesday 5 Dec. 1978**
    In the morning I picked up the Nj and the ṭulū‘ was gone through the
third time at the home of Ḥsēn ‘Āyid, at 8.00 a.m. I then started packing
to leave for Elat. At 11.30, just as I was about to go, the doctor
turned up, and with him, to my delight, Lezer. (They are so short of men
in Naxl that he can't patrol, but just has to take a lift when he can get
one.) We had a good talk about the local problems, and I learnt to my
relief that there are no plans for moving the Bedouin out of the area, tho'
an army camp (under Lezer probably) will be set up in Thamad. The teacher
Muṣṭafa intended to go back to al-‘Arīś today, but, to my delight, Lezer
refused to allow it. Muṣṭafa foolishly allowed the children out of school
when the doctor came, and so his visit became a farce, since almost all the
chiddren invented aches and pains and asked to be examined.
    I drove down to Elat on my own, carrying a sack of ṣadaqah flour for
Sālim b. Ḥ. Slēm, which I delivered to Mrāx.

**6 Dec. 1978 to 23 Jan 1979**
    In Elat for a few days, then to Jerusalem, where I stayed first in
the Truman Inst. flat on French Hill, and then, from early Jan., with
Marsia. Did some work with Blanc, wrote up field notes, and produced an
elaborate/application for the Israel Foundations Trustees, but the main
thing was that I wrote almost the whole of Cases and Texts III, on SH vs
Ṣāliḥ re ḥagg al-bēt. Worked only moderately well. Had a lot done on the
jeep, and spent a ‘week with the Hoffers in Haifa while it was being done.
