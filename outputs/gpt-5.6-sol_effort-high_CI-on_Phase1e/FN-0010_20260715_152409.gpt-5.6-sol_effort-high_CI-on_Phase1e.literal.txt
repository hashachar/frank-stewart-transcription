**Inheritance and marriage**  Slēm Ṣāliḥ (AS, X, II) 28 Nov 78
    The marriages of Slēm's siblings took place in the following order:
1. Salmān exchanged Fuṭūm, who was taken by Sālim ‘Aṭiyyih (A‘A), for the
    latter's sister, Fāṭmih. Salmān's father Ṣāliḥ was already dead by this
    time, so he acted his sister's willy. Neither of the
    marriages was a success - there were beatings and daxals - and in the end
    there was a simultaneous double divorce, which took place at the home of
    Fāṭmih's brother Silmiy.
2. Salmān exchanged Fuṭūm for H. Najm's daughter "‘Odiy". Slēm could add
    (or would add) nothing to what I already know.
3. Salmān married ‘Idih Slēmān (AS,X,II), whose willy was her
    father. He demanded 5 camels, which, said Slēm,was given. Really? I asked.
    Well, he said, he was given one pure bred camel, which is an acceptable
    substitute: yā tsūg xams sin tuṭrud sinn, yā tsūg naḍwah. Salmān did this
    because he could not fix a badal.
4. Slēm married Sālmih Ḥasan (AS,X,I). He paid 80 pounds to her father.
5. Fuṭūm married Slēmān Slēm (AS, X, I/3). He paid only IL 300 siyāg.
    Slēm says that all three brothers had to agree to the marriage.
6. Mḥa married ‘Idih ‘Iliy Slēmān (Dw) by siyāg. Slēm asked his brother how
    much, and Mḥa answered that it was not much. He consulted his brothers
    before marrying.

    Slēm says that as a child his family was mostly round Mśayyis. They
drank the water, just as they ate the very poor food that they then had
(jirīśih etc.). People had stronger stomachs in those days, he says. He
himself has subsequently moved about a good deal: he's spent.time both over
in the Bīr Mālḥih area and in Kuntillih (which I think is very rare among
the Xanāṭlih, at least among the younger ones, though Ḥsēn Ibn Jim‘ih was
also there with his bēt some time(s) long ago). At the moment he waters
at Thamad. I think he has 9 sheep and 10 goats/ (Incl. his mother's flock) He says he has two she-camels:
one of them waters at Bīr Mālḥih, the other is round here, and at the
moment he is waiting for it to come inx to water. He i says he will probably
go to live in W. Mrāx with his family. If he does sox, I think he will be
the first Xanṭaliy to set up home there. (He says so.) He says he would not
put up a hut near the Najamāt, but rather lower down the wadi, near the
hut presently used by the Xanāṭlih. He was very emphatic about the enmity
between Najamāt and Xanāṭlih.

    When Slēm's father died, Salmān was about twenty. This was in the Egypt-
ian period. He became seriously ill immediately after - Slēm gave the impres-
sion that the shock of his father's death was the cause, and even that it
was psychosomatic. He had stomach pains. His mother made a xunnah (compress?)
from gazelle dung which cured him, though I think he also got medical treat-
ment in Suez. Some or all of the younger children also fell ill soon
