Field notes        18

**Monday 27 Nov. 1978**
    Up at 7.00 to hear talk re mdamah of women, and also re W. al-Bēḍ̄. The
question was, will the other members of the mdamah help the Gṣār in any
problems they may have with Farrāj ‘Id (in particular, in meeting costs).
This is not explicitly covered by the mdamah, but the parties present agreed
to help the Gṣār (only Ḥ. Najm was absent in fact).
    The position between the Aḥaywāt and the Tarabīn is kull xamsih b-xamsit-
ha. This was confirmed explicitly some time before I came into the field.
There was a dispute between a rub‘ of the Aḥaywāt and one of the Tarabīn
about ḥaśīś smuggling, and in the course of it Ḥ. Najm and Farrāj ‘Id consul-
ted as-Sūfiy as to the relations between the two tribes.(SH says that ‘Id
Abu Ġraygānih in Gaza is the one to ask about this dispute.) This means that
Farrāj could make the three badwihs and then start seizing the Hs
camels, and no-one would have the right to intervene. If someone did
step in to help the Ḥs, then that groups camels would also be open to seizure,
which would be all to Farrāj's advantage, since it is easier to
seize the camels of several groupsthan of one (you have more chance of
finding them).
    At 9.30 we left with Ibn Mismiḥ, went to his home and there had lunch.
Mansiy turned up while we were there. We then made for sēl al-Jihayyir,
dropped Farrāj, and eat again, with śēx Slēmān and ‘Iliyān. We picked
up a tire we'd brought there (it had falled off SI's lorry without him
noticing it), looked unsuccessfully for SI, went in to Thamad to see if the
Red Cross had turned up (Michel Zuffery was expected, I'd talked to him
on the phone in Elat), and then to Ṭarfa for the night.


**Tuesday 28 Nov. 1978**
    Into Thamad at 8.00 and read in jeep. At 11.00 the doctor arrived, and
only with great difficulty did I and others manage to persuade Muṣṭafa the
teacher not to go back to al-‘Arīś with him. He's only been here a week,
but already has excellent excuses for returning. Ḥsēn ‘Āyid has two small
children with pneumonia, but he turned down the doctor's proposal to
take them to al-‘Arīś as he turned down mine to take them to Elat a couple
of days ago. The doc. gave him tablets, and they pulled through.
    Frayjih, SH's wife, had come in to see the doc. for back trouble this
morning. I took her home, since SH was busy, and with her some water, and
then Slēm "Gaṃṃāṛ" Ṣāliḥ (AS,X) a load of water to his home,
some 3kms from Thamad in Abu Suwwān. It's the first time I've done this
though he's been begging me to for ages. I then sat with him till 6.00
in the clinic and got the data on t9ff. He went up the wadi to Ḥsēn ‘Āyid's
home (say 2 km from Thmad or less) for dinner, and SH, Ḥsēn Haykal, Muṣṭafa
and I had our meal in Thamad.
