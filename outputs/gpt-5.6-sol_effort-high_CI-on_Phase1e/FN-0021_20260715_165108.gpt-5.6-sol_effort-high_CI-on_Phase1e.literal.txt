    (3) Slēmān b. Sa‘ad was recently in W. Mrāx (I think his wife or
baby were ill and he had to go down to Elat for that reason - I saw him
with them there) and told ‘Id ‘Iliyān that the Gs had bawwaged the Ḥs to
al-‘Urḍ̄iy when the latter visited them recently after having failed to get
the Ḥs to a bēt malamm. Somehow this came up this evening, and a three
sided meeting will be arranged between Ḥs, Gs, and Tarabīn (at which the
question of W. al-Bēd̄ per. se will be magrūṭ al-ḥaṣāh). The possible outcomes
are: (a) If the Gs are lying, and did bawwag the Ḥs prematurely (dūn śhūd
    al-‘adam), then the Ḥs will get a ḥagg.
    (b) If the Ḥs invented the story, then the Gs get a ḥagg.
    (c) And so also if the Tarabīn invented the story.
    SH strongly suspects that Farrāj is getting money on the side from
the Ḥs.
    At the end of the evening, when the Turbanis were already in their
jeep, Sālim Slēman (Ns) went to have a few words with Mha b. Salāmih. The
latter is one of the three daxala of Sālim's wijh in the dispute, and he
had agreed to yungul ad-daxal on condition that his two co-daxīls (both
Tihis) did so. This they did, so he is now stuck with it. Apparently he
has got worried at the sight of all the trouble Farrāj is having, and
made some attempt to unstick himself; but he can't..

**Sunday 3 Dec. 1978**
    Ṣadagah day. Farrāj Slēmān and his wife returned from Elat - they had
been there because she had had a sudden retention of urine, SH took them
to the airport the other evening in the jeep and from there they were
taken into Elat. However, she wasn't properly cured, and on Tue. Dec.5
they went off to al-‘Arīś for further treatment.
    I spent the day running around, e.g. taking some flour etc home for
Ḥsēn ‘Āyid. The Nj first decided to do the ṭulū‘ of SS (Sālim Slēmān, Ns)
today, then changed their mind. Ḥ. Swēlim said that if they didn't do it
today, he wouldn't agree; they said to hell with him. Slēmān Ḥamdān has
gone back to somewhere round Naxl today, since transport was available,
but is entirely agreeable to the ṭulū‘. The only problematic one is Swēlim;
it's far from clear what the reasons for his objections are, and it seems
his wanting the ceremony today was sheer perversity - he'd probably have
objected again. ‘Iliyān says he'll come back to the Nj sooner or later.
