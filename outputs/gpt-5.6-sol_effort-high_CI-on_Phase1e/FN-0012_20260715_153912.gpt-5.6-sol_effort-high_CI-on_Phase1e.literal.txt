**Gṣāṛ mdamah of women**  SH   30 Nov 78
    The members of this mdamah are all the women who belong to the descent
groups in the mdamah (Gṣāṛ, Ms., some Ns, H. Najm), but not women who have
married into it from outside. It seems that the Ḥs have long had a female
mdamah, but that none of the other lineages have one. The idea that the
Gṣ should have one was proposed by Ḥamdān ‘Id first, and then, independently,
by SH. The latter says that anything proposed by Ḥamdān automatically gets
objected to, and it was partly this that led Faṛṛāj Slēmān to object to
the idea. He was only convinced to join on 27 Nov., when we were in Kuntillih.
    The system works like this. Assume A has one female agnate, B has two,
and C has three, and that one of C's daughters beats another woman. C has to
pay one quarter of the resulting gaṣṣ; the remainder is divided into five
parts, of which C has to pay two. For these purposes only girls who have
reached puberty (the age of 16, as SH put it) are counted. I assume - tho'
I forgot to ask - that the system works the same way if payments are made
for a beating of one of the women.
    The system of kifīls is the same as in the male mdamah. Each sub-group
within the Gs has an elder who acts as kifīl for that sub-group vis-a-vis
every other Gs sub-group: e.g. SH is kifīl of the Salamāt, and if money was
due to Ḥamdān ‘Id and SI was refusing to pay it, then Ḥamdān would turn to
SH as guarantor. However, if I am not mistaken, Faṛṛāj Slēmān is the guarantor
of each of the three non-Gs groups in the mīdamah, so that if, say,
the Ms complained that SI wasn't paying up, they would turn to Faṛṛāj, and
he would turn to SH. Equally, Faṛṛāj is guarantor to each of the Gs sub-group
for each of the three outside groups: so that if the Ms weren't paying SI
what was due to him, the Salamāt would turn to Faṛṛāj.

**Kifīl terminology**
A is **kifīl ‘a** B, A guarantees that B will do something
A is **kifīl il** C, A guarantees to C (that B will do something)
**ana kifilt lak**, I guarantee you(that B will do something)

**śa‘‘it ad-damm**
    The word **śa‘‘ih** is related to the verb **śa‘‘**, **i**, which is used in two
ways: (1) śa‘‘ al-xaḅaṛ min tāwih ‘arab flān in fīh ḥurṃah śākiyih,'news came
    from the such-and-such that advances had been made on one of
    their women.'The verb is used only of news of a serious "crime",
    e.g. killing, advances on a woman; it would not be used of news
    of a minor dispute. And is used only of news which has just come
    in.
    (2) ana śa‘‘ayt al-xaḅaṛ,'I spread the news,'where the news would be
    of the kind indicated, and where the speaker is not one of the
    parties involved in the "crime", or at least, not a woman on whom
    advances were made (though he might be her brother).
