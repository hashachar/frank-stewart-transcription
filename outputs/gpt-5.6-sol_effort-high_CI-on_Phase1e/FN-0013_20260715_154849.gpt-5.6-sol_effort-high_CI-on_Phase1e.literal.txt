**An eye for an eye**
    The general principle seems to be that if you retaliate in kind for an
offence committed against you, you have to pay double the penalty that the
original offender pays. For instance, if someone rapes your sister, and
you then rape his, you will get manśad and jīrih, and he will get two manśads
and two jīrihs. If ‘Iliyān Ḥamdān were to retaliate against the Ḥs by cutting
down fences round one of their fields, then he'd have to pay ḥāggen to their
ḥagg.

    The obvious exception is the case of beatings and killings. Are there
others?

**Xamsih, ‘āylih, and rub‘**
    I put to SH the problem raised on 5/368, and he wasn't suprised by it.
The term xamsih is used, e.g. both for the Xanāṭlih as a whole (al-xanāṭlih
xamsih waḥdih ‘ā najamāt)* and for the lineages within the Xanāṭlih.
The whole Gṣāṛ mdamah is a single xamsih, but such groups as the Salamāt
are also xamsihs. I suppose the Ahaywāt are also a xamsih vis-a-vis the
outside by virtue of their mdamah, but I suppose a single bēt is not per
se a xamsih, though it can be that a xamsih consists of a single bēt,
e.g. Ḥamdān ‘Id within the Gṣāṛ (the ‘Yēdāt ‘Id).
    Clearly, every CLG is a xamsih. But the converse is not true:
the Salamāt are a xamsih as well. But here it can be argued that
they are a sub-CLG within the Gṣāṛ mdamah. However, even sub-groups,
genealogically defined, among the Ḥsēnāt, for instance, are called xamsihs,
and I don't think they are sub-CLGs - tho' I'm by no means certain.
    Who counts as being in the xamsih? If my little son or my sister is
killed, what happens?
    Now the analysis on 5/368 makes quite clear that the question of whether
I am ṭāli‘ al-xamsih ‘innak, is quite different from the question of whether
or not we are in the same xamsih, or to put it more exactly: of whether there
are two distinct xamsihs such that I belong to one, and you belong to the other. Some-
one in line b on 5/368 might well be considered in a different
xamsih from someone in line a.
    The question of whether I am ṭāli‘ al-xamsih from you is not only sig-
nificant in cases of killings. On tape 49, the Gṣāṛ madamah of ,
the question arises of whether Sālim ‘Id has to ṭili‘ from the Ns before
entering the Gs mdamah. He says that he does not, since he is ṭāli‘ al-xamsih.
Even before he said this on the tape, SH who was explaining to me, had
already said this to me.
