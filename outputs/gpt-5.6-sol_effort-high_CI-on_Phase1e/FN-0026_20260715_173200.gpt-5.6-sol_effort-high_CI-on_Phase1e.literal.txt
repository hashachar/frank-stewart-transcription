**Friday 26 Jan. 1979**
    Up at 7.00 and sit with Dax and write from him. He and I are a
long way from his home - of goats hair and canvas - with a naxabūr
between us and it. He has several good places for mag‘ad̄s in the ṭarfa.
No problem of firewood round here. The two homes are very close to
each other.
    Dax was extremely helpful and open with me. Apart from the data
which are set out below, I got from him - at last - a pretty full
genealogy of the Awlād Ṣāliḥ. Only the ‘Urjān (Jim‘ih b. Ġayθ's group)
are not adequately covered. At last I have the Najamāt almost
completely done (and for the Xanāṭlih there remains only to do some
more work on the Darawśih). Information on the other three clans is still
scrappy.
    After lunch we went into Bīr Mālḥih. I'd been told by Ḥsēn that one
person I could rely on entirely in the area is Sālim Najm (Ḥs), who has
a shop there. I left some of my jerry-cans with him. The attitude of the
Bīr Mālḥih people was altogether very friendly.
    We then went to Dax's other home, where were also Bi‘aybiś's son,
a bright boy called and Slēman, son of the late Lḥa Ṣāliḥ. Sulūm,
mother of z Dax's son in law ‘Id Sālim, was also there, dying.
    To sleep at 10.30.

**Saturday 27 Jan. 1979**
    Up at 7.00. Dax is also a late riser - first Bedouin I know who is
(and it's maybe exacerbated at the moment by a bad toothache he's got).
Went into Mālḥih in time to get the kids to school at 8.00, and then spent
three hours thoroughly cleaning the two batteries in the jeep. Had tea
in the shop with Sālim and Slēman Najm, and then went off with Dax and
Śēx Faraj of the Frā‘iyyih to get petrol (‘Abdallah didn't have any at
the time). Up to now the day had been windy, threatening rain, but it
suddenly cleared. Faraj is a very pleasant, cheerful character, though I
later learnt that he had recently lost three (I think) daughters from
diphtheria. Dax says he was originally put in as his, Dax's, deputy.
    We went to a place some 30 minutes from Mālḥih to the SW (I think),
called Bīr Hijiyyih*. Here there is a shop which belongs to Xd̄ēr, who, like
virtually the others in the area, belongs to the Frā‘iyyih. In the shop was
one of the Badāṛah, a man called Jim‘ih, missing one leg, but getting
around pretty well with a crude wooden crutch. He is either a
partner or employee of Xd̄ēr, and he did indeed have some petrol, as well
a good stock of other things (much better th:n the Thamad shop). There
were a few other men sitting around, and we sat and drank tea.
