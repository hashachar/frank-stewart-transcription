**Masāmiḥ vs. Nṣērāt re badal.** From SH, 23 Nov 78. Cf. 5/268.
    ‘Iliyān Ibin Mismiḥ has two daughters: Rayya’ (sic) and the younger
one, ‘Idih. Originally ‘Iliyān proposed exchanging ‘Idih for ‘Ālyih (sic),
but the Gṣāṛ demanded Rayya’. ‘Iliyān explained that she was destined to be
exchanged for a brid for her brother Slimān, but in the end he agreed:
as set out in 5/268 at the beginning.
    Now (according to ‘Iliyān Ibin Mismiḥ, reported once or twice removed
by SH) Sālim ‘Id (Nṣ) proposed to young Slimān ‘Iliyān that he "" young
‘Id Faṛṛāj. Sālim ‘Id said he'd pay IL 50,000 of the diyih.
    ‘Iliy ‘Id is also said to have proposed that Rayya’ take a ""
and kill ‘Id, or alternatively poison him, on the day of the wedding.
    The Nṣ thought the marriage was imminent, and they told Slimān to flee
with his sister and put her in the home of Sālim Sallām. They offered ‘Abd
ar-Ra‘ūf, the man who comes in a jeep to deal with pensions etc., IL 200 to
take the girl to Sālim, who lives at the moment some way above Kuntillih.
After some thought ‘Abd ar-Ra‘ūf refused. He later more or less confirmed
the story.
    ‘Iliyān Ibin Mismiḥ then went to the Gṣāṛ, explained the problems he
was having, and got their agreement to accept ‘Idih after all. The groom
to be remains Mismiḥ.
    The Nṣ now went to young Slimān and said, "Give us your sister Rayya’
for Mismiḥ ‘Id, and take in exchange Sālim ‘Id's daughter ‘Idih." Slimān
refused, and said, it seems as a joke, for hum taffaraw, "‘Idih ma‘ay", I
have ‘Idih's gaṣalah. The Nṣ wadda‘aw.
    Slimān then went to Sallām, son of Sālim Sallām, and they exchanged
sisters.
    Sālim ‘Id then set off to yixaṭab min ‘Iliyān, to make a further effort
to get Rayya’ for Mismiḥ ‘Id. He met ‘Iliyān on his way to the well. When
he heard the news of Rayya’ being given to Sallām, Sālim adxal Rayya’ ‘a
QalāOih mn-al-Gṣāṛ, and maybe xaṭṭ 9. He said, "Ana abda mjn Abu Ġraygānih."
    Slimān, on hearing of this, told his father ‘Iliyān of all that had
happened between him and the Nṣērāt.
    On hearing that Slimān had done this, Sālim ‘Id began demanding a manśad
from ‘Iliyān for Slimān having said "‘Idih ma‘ay." ‘Iliyān said he was
makbūr - SI being his kibīr - but xaṭṭ 3: ś. Slimān, SI, and H. Najm. Sālim
‘Id refused, and then ‘Iliyān xaṭṭ Ibin Jim‘ih, Ibin ‘Āyid, and one other.
At the same time ‘Iliyān says he has claims on the Nṣ for their actions.
    SH now wants to go to Kuntillih as soon as Faṛṛāj gets back from al-
‘Arīś,in order to settle the matter. Unlike some other Gṣāṛ, he wants the Nṣ
to stay in the mdamah - but to pay a ḥagg. He is very keen that the case
between Ms and Ns should not be heard by an outside judge, e.g. Ibin ‘Āyid.
